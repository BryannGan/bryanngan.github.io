MIROS — Medical Image to Reduced-Order Simulation

Open Source

End-to-end pipeline that produces patient-specific hemodynamic simulations directly from volumetric angiography in minutes. Integrates ML-based segmentation with 0D/1D Navier–Stokes solvers in SimVascular.

  • Automated pipeline from medical images to blood flow simulation results
  • Automatic boundary condition tuning via CMA-ES optimization
  • 1D Navier–Stokes + 0D lumped-parameter solvers
  • Interactive GUI for cardiac inflow waveform design
  • Cross-platform support (Windows, Linux, macOS)

Cardiac & Vascular Finite Element Analysis

Finite element simulation of cardiac and vascular mechanics using svFSI and FEBio, including fluid–structure interaction and nonlinear tissue constitutive models.

  • Patient-specific cardiac mechanics simulations (svFSI, FEBio)
  • Fluid–structure interaction in large arteries
  • High-performance computing for large-scale multiphysics problems

Physics-Informed Graph Neural Networks

Surrogate modeling of hemodynamic fields using graph neural networks that encode physical constraints, enabling fast inference on patient-specific vascular geometries.

  • GNN-based surrogate for 3D hemodynamic prediction
  • Physics-informed loss functions encoding conservation laws
  • Generalization across patient-specific anatomies

MCMC Parameter Estimation

Bayesian parameter estimation for patient-specific calibration of cardiovascular models, quantifying uncertainty in material properties and boundary conditions.

  • MCMC-based inference for tissue material parameters
  • Uncertainty quantification for boundary condition calibration
  • Integration with reduced-order models for computational efficiency
