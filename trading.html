Opening Range Momentum / Reversal

Deployed

Bryan investigated whether the first intraday bar carries a tradeable directional signal across two timeframes. A multi-phase validation pipeline was designed to control for overfitting and select only statistically robust configurations.

  • Multi-phase validation: nested splits, bootstrap CI filtering, outlier-dependence analysis
  • 11 of 49 candidates survived all statistical filters
  • Discovered that fixed configs outperform adaptive re-optimization—shaped deployment strategy
  • Identified exit engineering as the primary research frontier (97% of losers showed favorable excursion)
  • Currently deployed and trading live

Candlestick Rejection + Momentum Extremes

Edge Found

After the standalone rejection signal failed, Bryan hypothesized that adding a regime filter would rescue the signal. This combination strategy was the result.

  • Research progression: standalone failure motivated combination hypothesis
  • 9 of 97 tickers passed with statistically significant edge (p = 0.0001)
  • Validated through jackknife stability checks and multi-year rolling evaluation

Candlestick Rejection (Standalone)

No Edge

Bryan tested whether dominant-wick candles predict short-term direction in isolation.

  • No directional content found across 99 tickers with multiple signal quality gates
  • Negative result directly motivated the successful combination strategy above

Mean-Reversion Dip

No Edge

Bryan tested whether intraday pullbacks from recent highs create mean-reversion opportunities.

  • Weak pre-cost signal eliminated by realistic transaction costs across 99 tickers
  • Reinforced cost modeling as a first-order design constraint for all subsequent strategies

Fair Value Gap Pairs

No Edge

Bryan investigated whether sequential fair value gaps carry predictive information about price continuation or reversal.

  • 25.8M gap pairs analyzed; favorable/adverse excursion ratio = 0.999 (indistinguishable from random)
  • Clean null result ruled out an entire class of gap-based signals

Liquidity Sweep

No Edge

Bryan designed a paired-delta methodology to isolate sweep-specific directional effects from confounders (time-of-day, volatility, VWAP displacement).

  • 2.5M+ observations with sufficient power to detect edges as small as 0.2 bps
  • Sweeps are real price events but carry no tradeable directional signal

Stochastic Mean-Reversion

No Edge

Bryan modeled intraday price as an Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process and iteratively refined the strategy across three research phases.

  • Each phase diagnosed specific failure modes and attempted targeted fixes
  • Signal-to-noise ratio insufficient to overcome costs at intraday timescales

VWAP Crossover

No Edge

Bryan tested whether VWAP crossings with divergence thresholds signal institutional flow.

  • Comprehensive evaluation across 41 tickers; no post-cost edge found
  • Consistent with the broader finding that single-indicator signals lack sufficient edge in liquid equities
